Fundamental Analysis and Technical Analysis

There are two major methods for analysing a stock. Those are –

  1. Fundamental Analysis
  2. Technical Analysis

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis involves the process of understanding a stock’s intrinsic value through the analysis of various factors, which includes everything starting from the overall economy and industry conditions to the financial strength including earnings, expenses, assets and liabilities and management of individual companies.

Fundamental analysis is an extensive process since it takes into account both macro factors like country’s economic and industrial conditions and microeconomic factors that can influence the price of stocks to facilitate a comprehensive analysis. The method uses several qualitative and quantitative factors to determine how good a stock is.

Some of the qualitative indicators studied in this method are:

  1. Management competency
  2. Competitive edge
  3. Business model and business cycles
  4. Corporate governance
  5. Growth
  6. Customer base

Some of the qualitative indicators studied in this method are:

  1. Company’s annual revenue
  2. Total profit
  3. Tangible assets
  4. Accounting ratios

Apart from these, a fundamental analyst must also be aware of the socio-economic and political factors that might influence a stock’s value in the future.

Interpretation

As per the fundamental analysis, if the intrinsic value of a stock is less than its market price then that security is undervalued. Alternately if the intrinsic value of a stock is more than its market price then that security is overvalued.

Investors who rely on this determination will then buy a stock if it is undervalued in the expectation to yield significantly higher returns in the long-run. Similarly they will sell a stock when it is overvalued since they think its prices will soon fall.

In short, fundamental analysis is an approach that is undertaken by investors and works on the principle of “ buy and hold”. Investors who go by fundamental analysis, base their decisions to buy a stock on a comprehensive understanding of a company and hold their investments for a longer period.

Technical Analysis

While a fundamental analysis of a stock accounts for an array of factors, technical analysis solely takes historical data directly related to the particular stock into account. That is the primary difference between fundamental analysis and technical analysis.

In short, technical analysis is predominantly based on patterned price movements. Technical analysts use various charts to identify patterns and market trends by utilising differing forms of technical chart types and other chart functions. Interpreting charts is very critical and requires in-depth knowledge about technical analysis.

There are three main types of technical analysis charts. They are all created using the same price data but display the data in different ways.

  • Candlestick
  • Bar
  • Line charts
